# Teardown: the Clicky launch (Apr–Jul 2026)

The worked example behind `launch-playbook`. A macOS AI companion launched by
@FarzaTV with a product-mascot account @heyclicky. ~11 weeks, two voices,
repeated 2k–14k-like flagship drops. Every number below is from the real posts.

## Why the two biggest hits won

**Voice control (14.2k)** and **dictation (9.9k)** both:
- Opened with a flat, huge claim ("control my computer with just my voice").
- Named the crowd and went further ("many products do dictation… so we shipped
  *screen-aware* dictation").
- Showed one primitive, live, with escalating stakes.
- Closed candidly ("it's a little buggy, but we're making it better every day").

## The hook, verbatim (dictation demo)

> "Today we're adding dictation… many products already do this exact thing
> today. But here's the thing: when I'm writing, I often don't know what I even
> want to say. So check this out — we're shipping screen-aware dictation."

That is the formula in three sentences: claim → name the crowd → "so we went
further."

## Reveal laddering

Pointer → on-screen drawing → user draw-to-direct (spatial context) →
screen-aware dictation. Each flagship shipped ONE new primitive that built on
the last. No drop bundled features.

## The mascot channel, in its own words

"i am being fixed" · "my creators are upgrading me" · "i wanna help you make
music" · "i went to demo day" · "i am celebrating $10k mrr at a gas station" ·
"based on a true story" · "see you tomorrow" (teaser). First person, lowercase,
feelings over announcements — the brand's charm lived here while the founder
account carried reach.

## The mascot channel's three repeating templates

Frame-level study of all 18 mascot/secondary clips shows the channel runs on
just three formats:

1. **Sprint update** — "everything we're shipping" with a 3–5 person cast
   passing one mic hand-to-hand, big synced captions, and the version number
   used as an on-camera prop (whiteboard + spoken "v1.0.11").
2. **Founder-solo narrative** — fixed outdoor seat, 1–2 screen-recording proof
   cutaways, always ending on a domain card.
3. **Bare feature demo** — a raw 4–15s screen recording, no cast, no narration
   ("drag your files in and start chatting").

Only ~3 of 9 clips per batch showed legible product UI — the rest are pure
personality/community content. That ratio is deliberate: the mascot channel's
job is affection and cadence, not information density.

## Frame-level craft observed

- Consistent "keynote" compositing: every app demo shown as an inset window over
  one warm backdrop → different apps read as one product.
- Karaoke captions, 3–6 word chunks, no box, synced to speech.
- State shown at OS chrome: the menu bar text flips "Listening" / "Speaking".
- Cold opens on the characteristic moment (a viewfinder overlay; hand-drawing on
  a chalkboard) — never a logo.
- Widescreen framed for a 9:16 crop so one shoot serves both channels.
- Candid "still buggy" closes instead of hard CTAs.
